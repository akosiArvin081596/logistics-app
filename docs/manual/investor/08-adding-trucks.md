# Adding Trucks to Your Fleet

You start with one truck (or whatever you initially funded). Over time, you may want to grow. This chapter is the process for adding additional trucks.

## Why add trucks

A few reasons investors expand:

- **Strong returns on the first truck** lead to confidence in scaling.
- **Available capital** to deploy productively.
- **Tax-advantaged depreciation** on additional assets.
- **Risk diversification** — multiple trucks insulate against a single-truck issue.

A few reasons to wait:

- **Inconsistent performance** on existing trucks.
- **Soft freight market.**
- **Liquidity constraints.**
- **Capital better deployed elsewhere.**

Discuss with LogisX leadership and your own financial advisor.

## The high-level process

Adding a truck involves several steps:

1. **Discussion with LogisX.** Confirm interest, timing, scale.
2. **Truck identification.** Either you find one, LogisX finds one, or a hybrid.
3. **Financing arrangement.** Capital from you; some structures involve LogisX or third-party financing.
4. **Purchase.** Title in your name (or LLC).
5. **Vehicle Lease signing** for the new truck.
6. **Truck registration in the system.**
7. **Driver assignment** (existing or newly hired).
8. **First load runs.**

Each step takes time. The whole process can run 4-12 weeks depending on truck availability, financing speed, and onboarding the driver.

## Step 1: Conversation

Message operations or call. Express your interest in adding a truck.

Topics to cover:

- **Number of trucks** you want to add.
- **Timeline** for adding.
- **Capital available** for the purchase.
- **Preferred equipment** (dry van, reefer, flatbed, specific year/make).
- **Driver preference** — do you want LogisX to source, or do you have a driver in mind?

Operations will tell you about:

- **Current freight market** and demand outlook.
- **Truck availability** (used market, new market, lead times).
- **Any specific opportunities** they're aware of (existing trucks for sale, drivers looking for trucks).
- **Capital and financing** options.

This conversation is exploratory. No commitments yet.

## Step 2: Finding the truck

Three paths:

**You find it.** You spot a used truck for sale on the market and want to purchase it. LogisX helps with the inspection and brings it into the fleet.

**LogisX finds it.** Operations has relationships with dealers and other carriers. They can source trucks matching your specs.

**Hybrid.** You and LogisX both look; the first promising find proceeds.

In any case, the truck needs to be:

- **In decent mechanical condition** (passes pre-purchase inspection).
- **Title-clean** (no liens, no salvage history unless disclosed).
- **Acceptable to LogisX** as fleet equipment.

## Step 3: Financing

How the capital comes together:

**All your capital.** You write a check for the full purchase price. Simplest path.

**Hybrid financing.** You put down a portion; the rest is financed through a lender. The truck title may have a lien from the lender until paid.

**LogisX financing.** Less common, but possible in some structures. LogisX advances capital for the purchase; you pay back over time.

The Master Participation Agreement may have specific terms about how financing fits in. Refer to it.

## Step 4: Purchase

Standard truck purchase process. Inspection, title transfer, payment. The truck title ends up in your name or your LLC.

LogisX may help coordinate, but you're the buyer and titleholder. Have a lawyer review the paperwork if amounts are significant.

## Step 5: Vehicle Lease for the new truck

After purchase, you and LogisX sign a **new Vehicle Lease Agreement** specifically for this truck. The Vehicle Lease:

- Documents you as Lessor and LogisX as Lessee.
- Specifies the truck (VIN, year, make, model).
- Sets the terms (duration, fees, conditions).
- Inspects the condition at handover.

You sign electronically through the same Document Portal that holds your other agreements. The signed PDF appears in your portal.

## Step 6: Truck registration in the system

Operations adds the truck to the LogisX system:

- **Trucks page** → Add new truck.
- **VIN, plate, year, make, model** entered.
- **Owner ID** set to your investor record (so revenue and expenses attribute correctly).
- **Fixed cost fields** populated (insurance, ELD, IRP, HVUT, maintenance fund).
- **Documents uploaded** (registration, insurance, inspection — once they're in your name).

Once registered, the truck appears on your Fleet Breakdown.

## Step 7: Driver assignment

If you don't have a driver lined up:

- **LogisX hires** — typically from existing applications or new ones.
- **You suggest** — if you know a qualified driver looking for a truck.
- **Existing driver moves** — sometimes a driver moves to a new truck.

The driver undergoes the standard driver onboarding (documents, drug test, FMCSA Clearinghouse, training). Time: 1-3 days typically.

If a driver moves from another truck to yours, no onboarding needed — just an assignment change.

## Step 8: First load

Once truck and driver are set, the driver picks the first load. Revenue starts accruing. Your dashboard shows the new truck and its earnings.

The first month is typically a "learning curve" — the driver adapts to the new truck, dispatch learns the truck's quirks, etc. Don't expect peak performance immediately.

## Costs to anticipate

**At purchase:**
- Truck price.
- Inspection fees.
- Title transfer fees.
- Any registration / IRP setup.
- HVUT for the partial year.

**Recurring monthly:**
- Insurance.
- ELD subscription.
- IRP (annual / 12).
- HVUT (annual / 12).
- Maintenance fund reserve.

**Trip variable:**
- Fuel.
- Tolls.
- Repairs (occasional but inevitable).

The recurring costs accrue immediately. Revenue takes a few weeks to ramp.

## Cash flow timing

Some realistic expectations:

- **Month 1 after purchase:** revenue is low (driver onboarding, load mix takes time). Expenses still accrue. Net may be slightly negative.
- **Month 2:** revenue improves. Net likely positive but lower than steady-state.
- **Month 3 onwards:** steady-state performance, similar to your existing trucks.

Plan capital flow with this curve in mind. The first month or two can be light.

## Documentation per truck

Each new truck generates a new set of documents:

- **Vehicle Lease** (signed).
- **Exhibit A** (vehicle spec).
- **Truck records** in LogisX (registration, insurance, inspection).
- **Updated Master Agreement** (if any amendments needed).

All accessible from your Document Portal.

## Scaling beyond a few trucks

For investors growing to 3+ trucks, additional considerations:

- **Driver pool** — are there enough drivers, or are you competing with LogisX-owned trucks for available drivers?
- **Maintenance capacity** — does the LogisX maintenance schedule scale?
- **Financial reporting complexity** — multiple trucks make tax reporting more involved.
- **Liquidity** — is your capital tied up across multiple trucks?

These aren't blockers; just things to plan for.

## A note on speed

Adding a truck is not a fast process. Expect 4-12 weeks from "I want to add a truck" to "it's running its first load." The slowest steps are usually truck identification and driver hiring.

Don't make commitments based on a 4-week timeline; budget 8.

## What you can't do unilaterally

- **Force-add a truck to LogisX management.** It requires LogisX agreement.
- **Change the Master Agreement terms** to favor your new truck differently. Same terms apply to all your trucks (unless explicitly amended).
- **Bypass driver onboarding.** Every driver goes through the same flow.
- **Self-dispatch your trucks.** They run under LogisX management.

These limits are by design — they protect both you and LogisX.

## Pro tips

- **Run the numbers before committing.** Project revenue and costs for the first 6 months. Make sure it's a positive ROI scenario.
- **Don't add during slow seasons.** Late summer and winter are typically softer freight markets. Adding then puts you in a slow start.
- **Plan the timing with your taxes.** Section 179 deduction, depreciation strategies — work with your CPA.
- **Document everything.** Sale agreements, inspection reports, financing — keep paper copies.
