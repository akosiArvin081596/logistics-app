# Communicating with Operations

The in-app messaging connects you directly with LogisX operations. This chapter is when to message, what to expect, and how to make the most of the channel.

## Opening Messages

From your dashboard, scroll to or navigate to the **Messages** section. You'll see a chat window.

The thread shows your conversation history with operations. New messages from operations appear here in real time.

## What to message about

A few common reasons to reach out:

**Dashboard questions.** "Why is my April revenue lower than March?" "What does this $400 repair charge cover?" The dashboard math is transparent but specific numbers sometimes need explanation.

**Truck observations.** If you have direct knowledge about your truck (you've visited the yard, talked to the driver, etc.), share it. "Driver mentioned the AC is acting up." Operations might not have heard yet.

**Report requests.** "Can you generate a custom report covering Q1 with year-over-year comparison?" The standard PDF report is fine for most purposes; for special asks, operations can prepare one.

**Strategic questions.** "Thinking about adding a second truck — what's the freight market looking like?" Operations has visibility into trends you may not.

**Document needs.** "Can you re-send my tax K-1 to my accountant's email?"

**Anything else** that needs a response from the LogisX team.

## What not to message about

**Day-to-day operational decisions.** Which load to take, which driver to assign, when to do a pickup — those are operations' calls, not yours. You'll see the results in your dashboard.

**Driver-specific complaints.** If you've heard something about your driver, share factually but don't direct operations on what to do. Personnel decisions are their domain.

**Tax advice.** LogisX provides the data; your CPA interprets. Don't expect operations to walk through your tax return.

**Legal counsel.** Contract questions are fine; legal advice isn't. For deep legal questions, your attorney.

## Response times

Typical:

- **Business hours, weekdays:** minutes to a couple of hours.
- **Evenings, weekends:** delayed; non-urgent messages may wait until Monday.
- **Holidays:** longer delays.

If something is urgent and time-sensitive, message with "URGENT" or call directly (operations has a phone line they'll share with you).

For routine questions, expect same-day or next-business-day response.

## Tone

Operations communicates professionally. Match that. A few specifics:

**Be specific.** "My April earnings look wrong" is hard to act on. "My April earnings show $1,200 but I expected $2,500 based on the loads I knew were running on TRK-101 — can you walk me through the math?" is actionable.

**Quote the dashboard.** Screenshots help. So do specific line items from your breakdown.

**Be patient with explanations.** The first response might explain the data; if you still have questions, follow up. Don't expect a single message to resolve everything.

**Be respectful.** Operations is the team running your fleet day to day. They're on your side. Treat them like teammates, not vendors.

## Real-time updates

The message thread updates in real time. When you send something, operations sees it within seconds. When they reply, you see it within seconds (if you have the dashboard open).

If your message is read but not replied to within a reasonable window, follow up with a brief check-in. Don't assume they're ignoring you — usually they're working on the answer.

## Attaching photos or documents

Tap the paperclip icon in the message bar. Pick a photo or file from your device.

Common attachments:

- **Screenshot of a dashboard number** you're asking about.
- **Photo from a yard visit** if you noticed something.
- **Document** from your end (e.g., a tax form from your accountant with a question).

Keep file sizes reasonable — a few MB. Large files take longer to upload.

## Privacy

Your messages with operations are private to your account and the LogisX team. Other investors don't see your messages. Drivers don't see them.

That said, LogisX administrators can read the audit-side. Treat the channel as work communication, not personal.

## When to use phone

A few scenarios where phone beats messaging:

- **Time-sensitive situations** that need back-and-forth.
- **Complex contract or financial questions** that benefit from voice.
- **Sensitive conversations** (concerns, disputes).
- **Anything where you'd rather hear tone of voice.**

LogisX operations has a phone number you can call. Use it when text isn't enough.

## After-hours coverage

If you message after hours and it's urgent, the typical pattern is:

1. **Send the message** describing the urgency.
2. **Call the after-hours line** if appropriate.
3. **Don't expect routine messages to be answered overnight.**

For most investors, after-hours communication is rare. Operations runs during business hours.

## Records and history

Every message is preserved in the thread. You can scroll back as far as your account has been active. The history is useful for:

- **Re-reading explanations** of past dashboard questions.
- **Documenting agreements** ("Operations confirmed X on date Y").
- **Tracking issues** that span weeks.

If you need a specific message but can't find it, search by keyword.

## Setting communication expectations

Some investors are high-touch (frequent questions, lots of conversations); others are low-touch (occasional check-ins). Either is fine.

Communicate your preferences:

- "I'd like a weekly status check-in" — operations can schedule.
- "I'll only message when I have specific questions" — operations won't pester.
- "I want to be informed of any maintenance over $1,000" — operations will alert.

A clear understanding upfront beats inconsistent expectations.

## Escalating beyond operations

Most issues resolve with operations directly. If they don't:

1. **Talk to your operations contact directly by phone.** Email and chat can lose nuance.
2. **Escalate to LogisX leadership.** Each investor has a relationship with a senior contact — typically the CEO (Deshorn King) or a designated manager.
3. **Refer to your contract.** The Master Agreement defines dispute resolution if direct conversation doesn't work.

Escalation is rare. Most things resolve in conversation.

## Feedback

Operations welcomes feedback. About:

- **The platform** — features you'd like, things that are confusing.
- **Communication** — what's working, what isn't.
- **Reporting** — what data you wish you had.
- **The relationship overall** — how you're feeling about your investment.

Feedback shapes the future. Don't be shy.

## Pro tips

- **Use the messages thread instead of email when you can.** It's in the same context as your dashboard data. Operations can refer to both at once.
- **Don't fragment a conversation.** If you're discussing one topic, keep it in one thread. Don't open a new conversation for each thought.
- **Acknowledge responses.** Even a "thanks, that clears it up" lets operations know they answered correctly. Helps them help you better next time.
- **Save important responses.** Screenshot or copy. If operations confirms something significant, you want it findable.
