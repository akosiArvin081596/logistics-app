# Welcome

You've invested in the LogisX fleet. The truck(s) you fund are operated by professional drivers under LogisX management, generating revenue you share. This guide is everything you need to know about your investor dashboard, how to read the numbers, and how to stay connected to operations.

## What being a LogisX investor means

You own one or more trucks. LogisX manages them — finds the loads, hires the drivers, handles dispatch, deals with maintenance, manages compliance. You receive your share of the profit each truck generates.

The split is laid out in your Master Participation & Management Agreement (typically 50/50 of net profit, after expenses). Your dashboard shows exactly how that calculation works for each month and overall.

You are **not** an employee or a passive lender — you're an asset owner with professional management. That's reflected in:

- **Your ownership.** Each truck is in your name (or your LLC).
- **Your visibility.** The dashboard shows you everything happening on your fleet.
- **Your earnings.** Real profit-sharing, not a fixed return.
- **Your documents.** Each agreement you signed is in your portal.

## What this guide doesn't cover

- **Operations management** — load assignment, driver communication, day-to-day dispatch. That's LogisX operations' job, not yours.
- **Maintenance decisions** — when to repair, who to use. LogisX handles. You see the costs.
- **Financial accounting** — tax preparation, payroll. You handle on your side; we provide the data.

If you find yourself wanting to do operational work, talk to LogisX leadership about a different arrangement — that's a different relationship than investor.

## What to expect from this relationship

A few things you should expect:

**Transparency.** Every truck's revenue, expense, and pay calculation is visible on your dashboard. The math is shown in full. Nothing is hidden.

**Communication.** LogisX operations is reachable through the in-app chat and (for major matters) phone. Response times during business hours are minutes to hours; not days.

**Reporting.** You can download a PDF report any time covering any date range. Use it for your accountant, your records, or just understanding your investment.

**Honest accounting.** When something goes wrong — a damaged truck, a load loss, a slow month — you'll see it in the numbers. The system doesn't paper over bad weeks.

**Professional management.** Your trucks are run by experienced dispatch and driven by vetted drivers. The same standards apply to your trucks as to LogisX-owned ones.

## What we expect from you

A few things from your side:

**Read your contract.** The Master Participation & Management Agreement, your Vehicle Lease, your Exhibit A — they're the source of truth for your relationship with LogisX. Refer back to them when you have questions about the structure.

**Use the dashboard.** Sign in at least monthly. Reviewing your numbers keeps both you and LogisX accountable.

**Communicate questions.** If something doesn't make sense, ask. We'd rather answer 100 questions than have an investor confused or unhappy.

**Trust the process but verify.** The dashboard shows what's happening. Compare to your bank deposits and your tax forms. Things should reconcile.

**Be patient with bad months.** Trucking has cycles. A slow month isn't a crisis; sustained problems are. Communicate either way.

## A note on returns

Trucking returns aren't index-fund-like. You'll see months with strong profits and months with smaller (or, rarely, negative) returns. Over a year, the average matters more than any single month.

Factors that move returns up:

- High demand for freight (busy season, supply chain shocks).
- Good driver assignment to your trucks (productive drivers).
- Low fuel prices.
- Few maintenance issues.

Factors that move returns down:

- Slow freight market (post-holiday lulls).
- Major maintenance events (a transmission, an engine).
- High fuel prices.
- Driver-specific issues (sick driver, equipment failures).

LogisX manages what we can; some of it is outside anyone's control. The long-term average is what matters.
