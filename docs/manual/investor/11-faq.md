# Frequently Asked Questions

The questions investors ask most often.

## The investment itself

**Q. What am I buying when I invest in LogisX?**
You're buying one or more trucks, which LogisX operates under contract. You own the truck (title in your name or LLC); LogisX manages day-to-day operations.

**Q. What returns can I expect?**
Varies. Strong trucks in busy markets can generate 15-30% annualized return on capital invested. Slow markets or maintenance-heavy years can be lower. No guarantees — trucking has cycles.

**Q. What's the typical commitment period?**
The Master Participation & Management Agreement defines terms; typical durations are 3-5 years with extensions. Exit before then has terms in your contract.

**Q. What if I want to exit early?**
Refer to your Master Agreement. Typically involves notice period, asset disposition (sell truck back to LogisX, sell to third party, etc.), and final settlement of accounts.

**Q. Who decides which loads my truck runs?**
LogisX operations. You don't decide. The Master Agreement gives operational authority to LogisX.

**Q. Can I have a say in driver hiring?**
You can suggest drivers and discuss preferences with operations. Hiring decisions are made by LogisX leadership.

**Q. Can I visit my truck?**
Yes. LogisX is your business partner; the truck is your asset. Schedule visits with operations.

## Dashboard and reporting

**Q. How often does the dashboard update?**
Real-time. As loads close, expenses post, and statuses change, the dashboard reflects them within seconds.

**Q. Why is my dashboard sometimes slow?**
The system reads from Google Sheets, which has API rate limits. The system caches aggressively but bursts can cause brief slowness.

**Q. Can I see year-over-year comparisons?**
Not built-in. Generate reports for the same range across years and compare manually.

**Q. Can I export data to spreadsheet?**
Generate a PDF report and extract numbers manually. There's no direct CSV export currently.

**Q. Can I see the original load rate confirmations?**
Not from the dashboard. Operations has them; message to request.

**Q. The numbers on my dashboard don't match what I expected — what now?**
Click into the breakdown to see the underlying loads and expenses. Compare to your records. Message operations with specifics if still confused.

## Earnings and pay

**Q. How is my profit split with LogisX?**
Typically 50/50 of net profit, per your Master Agreement. Net profit = revenue minus all expenses (driver pay, fixed costs, trip expenses).

**Q. Can the split be different per truck?**
Theoretically yes if your contract has truck-specific terms. Generally no — same split applies across your fleet.

**Q. When do I get distributions?**
Typically the first week of the following month for the prior month's earnings. Direct deposit to the bank account you set up during onboarding.

**Q. Why is my distribution lower than my dashboard's "Your Earnings" line?**
Possible causes:
- Timing differences (expense approved after distribution calculated).
- Retained capital (some structures retain a portion for reserves).
- Tax withholding (rare but possible).

Check your bank deposit against the dashboard. Message operations if discrepancy.

**Q. What happens if my truck has a bad month and earnings are negative?**
Net negative is rare but possible. In a negative month, you don't get a distribution; the deficit may be carried forward against future profits. Your contract spells out exactly how this works.

**Q. What if revenue grew but expenses grew more?**
Net could shrink. Your dashboard shows it.

**Q. What if I want monthly statements?**
Generate a PDF report each month from the dashboard. It's your monthly statement.

## Fleet

**Q. How many trucks can I add?**
No formal limit. Practically, the constraint is operational capacity (drivers, dispatch attention, maintenance bandwidth).

**Q. What's the process for adding a truck?**
See the Adding Trucks chapter. Discussion, identification, financing, purchase, lease signing, registration, driver, first load.

**Q. Can I sell my truck back to LogisX?**
Discuss with leadership. The buy-sell terms depend on your situation.

**Q. What happens if my truck is in an accident?**
Insurance covers physical damage. LogisX coordinates repair or replacement. Lost-revenue periods (truck in shop) show in your dashboard as lower revenue for that period.

**Q. What if a driver damages my truck through negligence?**
Standard insurance covers most damage. Negligent damage may have driver-side consequences. LogisX handles the operational side; serious incidents may involve legal counsel.

**Q. Can I choose my driver?**
Influence yes, choose no. Operations assigns.

## Contract and legal

**Q. Where is my Master Agreement?**
Document Portal. Open and re-read periodically.

**Q. Can the Master Agreement be amended?**
Yes, by mutual agreement. Amendments are documented and signed.

**Q. What's the difference between the Master Agreement and the Vehicle Lease?**
- **Master Agreement** is the umbrella contract for your relationship with LogisX.
- **Vehicle Lease** is per-truck; documents you (Lessor) leasing the truck to LogisX (Lessee).

You sign one Master Agreement and one Vehicle Lease per truck.

**Q. Do I need a lawyer for the Master Agreement?**
Strongly recommended. The Agreement is a substantial document with long-term implications. Have a lawyer review before signing.

**Q. Can I assign my interest to someone else?**
Refer to your contract. Generally limited; transfers typically require LogisX consent.

**Q. What happens to my fleet if I die or become incapacitated?**
Refer to your contract and your own estate planning. Most arrangements have provisions for successors or estate execution.

## Taxes

**Q. Will I get a 1099 or K-1?**
Depends on your structure. Individuals and single-member LLCs typically get a 1099-NEC. Partnerships and multi-member LLCs get a K-1.

**Q. When does the 1099 arrive?**
Late January for the prior tax year.

**Q. Can I deduct truck expenses on my personal return?**
Most truck expenses are deducted from revenue before your distribution — they're already netted out. Talk to a CPA for specifics.

**Q. What about depreciation?**
Trucks are depreciable assets. Section 179 / bonus depreciation / MACRS apply. Talk to a CPA.

**Q. Should I form an LLC?**
Many investors find LLCs beneficial for liability and tax. Talk to a lawyer.

**Q. Should I pay quarterly estimated taxes?**
If you receive 1099 income, yes. Estimate based on your projected annual liability.

## Growing concerns

**Q. What if the freight market crashes?**
Some pain in trucking. Your dashboard will show declining revenue. Communication from LogisX about strategy.

**Q. What if a key person leaves LogisX?**
Operations continues; ownership of the truck and the contract is preserved. Leadership transitions are typically planned.

**Q. What if regulations change?**
LogisX adapts. Drivers may need new certifications; trucks may need upgrades. Communicated as it happens.

**Q. What if I disagree with a LogisX decision?**
Talk to operations. Escalate to leadership. Refer to your contract for dispute resolution.

That's the standard FAQ. For anything more specific, message operations.
