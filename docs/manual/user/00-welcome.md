# Welcome to LogisX

This is the user manual for the LogisX dispatch and fleet management platform. Whether you are a driver behind the wheel, a dispatcher coordinating loads, a super admin managing the business, or an investor who owns one of the trucks, this guide walks you through everything the app can do for you.

## What this manual covers

The manual is organized by role. Find your role in the table of contents, jump to that chapter, and read top-to-bottom — you will see the same screens you see in the app, with step-by-step instructions for every workflow you'll touch.

| Role | Chapter | What you'll learn |
|---|---|---|
| **Driver** | Chapter 2 | How to accept loads, report GPS, update status, upload proof of delivery, and message dispatch. |
| **Dispatcher** | Chapter 3 | How to assign loads, monitor the fleet, manage expenses, and keep operations running. |
| **Super Admin** | Chapter 4 | How to manage users, review applications, run financial reports, and use the admin tools. |
| **Investor** | Chapter 5 | How to view your fleet's earnings, download reports, and access your signed documents. |

Three chapters cover scenarios that don't require an account at all:

- **Chapter 6 — Public Workflows.** How drivers apply for work, how investors apply to fund a truck, and how customers track a load with just a load ID.

The last two chapters — **Troubleshooting** and **FAQ** — answer the questions that come up most often, regardless of role.

## What this manual does NOT cover

For technical details — server architecture, API endpoints, database schema, deployment — see the companion **Technical Documentation** PDF. This manual is written for the people who use the app, not the people who run the servers.

## Conventions used

Throughout the manual:

- **Bold text** highlights buttons, menu items, and field labels you'll see on screen.
- `Monospace text` indicates literal values you type (load IDs, URLs).
- Screenshots show the app as it appears at the time of publication. If your screen looks slightly different, the platform may have been updated — the underlying workflow should be the same.
- Callouts marked **Note** or **Warning** highlight things that are easy to miss or get wrong.

## Getting help

If you're stuck and the manual doesn't answer your question:

- **Drivers** — contact your dispatcher first; they can see your status, your loads, and the system as a whole.
- **Dispatchers and Admins** — contact your operations lead or the technical team.
- **Investors** — contact LogisX operations using the email link in your dashboard's footer.

Now turn the page and find your role. Welcome aboard.
