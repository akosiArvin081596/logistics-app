# Welcome

You're a dispatcher at LogisX — the person who connects loads to drivers and keeps the fleet moving. This guide is everything you'll touch in the app on a daily, weekly, and as-needed basis.

## What dispatch does

The dispatcher role is the operational center of LogisX. Loads arrive from brokers — usually automatically from emailed rate confirmations, sometimes manually. You assign them to drivers, track the trucks live, handle status corrections, manage expense submissions, and communicate with the drivers throughout. When something goes off-script, you're the first responder.

You report to Super Admin and escalate financial or policy decisions to them. They handle invoice approvals, user management, and the things that touch money or contracts. You handle the rest.

## What this guide covers

- **Getting started** — your home screen, the four dashboard tabs, the basic flow of a day.
- **The job board** — where unassigned loads come from and how to assign them.
- **Reassignment** — moving loads between drivers mid-trip.
- **The tracking map** — reading live driver positions and ETAs.
- **Status management** — when geofencing isn't enough.
- **Expenses** — reviewing what drivers submit; logging on their behalf.
- **Messaging** — chat with drivers, scoped to specific loads when useful.
- **Customer tracking** — sharing the public tracker URL with brokers and shippers.
- **Notifications** — the catch-all alert feed.
- **Fleet view** — trucks, trailers, who's available.
- **Best practices** — habits that make the job easier.
- **Troubleshooting and FAQ** — every weird situation and how to handle it.

## What this guide doesn't cover

- **Hiring and firing** — that's Super Admin (review applications, approve/reject).
- **Pay and invoices** — Super Admin approves invoices. You'll see expenses but not weekly pay decisions.
- **System configuration** — user accounts, role changes, data integrity tools — all Super Admin.
- **Customer pricing and broker negotiations** — handled outside LogisX or by leadership.

If you find yourself wanting to do one of those things, escalate to Super Admin rather than trying to do it yourself.

## How to read this guide

Each chapter is self-contained — you can jump to the one you need. New dispatchers should read top to bottom on day one. Experienced dispatchers can use it as a reference and skim only what's new.

The screenshots show the dashboard as it appeared at publication. If your screen looks slightly different, the platform has been updated; the workflow is the same.

## A note on tempo

Dispatching is a job with bursts of activity and stretches of calm. New loads arrive in clusters in the morning. Drivers report status throughout the day. Issues come up unpredictably. Some days you'll be answering five messages at once; other days you'll be watching a few loads roll uneventfully across the country.

The app is designed for both modes. It updates in real time when something happens — you don't have to refresh — and it stays out of your way when nothing does. The right rhythm is to glance at the dashboard a few times an hour during active periods and respond to anything that needs you.

## A note on tone with drivers

You're the voice of the company to every driver. They see your messages, your reassignment decisions, your status corrections. They notice the tone.

Be direct, clear, and respectful. Acknowledge what they're dealing with. Don't talk down. Don't blame. When you have to deliver bad news (a cancelled load, a refusal of an expense), do it cleanly and explain why.

Drivers who feel respected work harder and stay longer. The whole company benefits.
