# Messaging Drivers

The Messages screen is the day-to-day communication channel with every driver in your fleet. This chapter is how to use it well.

## Opening Messages

Sidebar → **Messages**. You'll see a list of conversations, one per driver. Each entry shows:

- Driver name.
- Last message preview.
- Unread count badge.
- Timestamp of last activity.

Click a conversation to open the chat thread.

## The chat interface

Standard chat layout:

- **Driver's messages** appear on the left.
- **Your messages** on the right.
- **Timestamp** for each message.
- **Read indicator** — you can see whether the driver has read your latest.
- **Message box** at the bottom — type and tap Send.
- **Attachment** (paperclip icon) — for photos.

Conversations are live. New messages appear without refreshing.

## Per-load messaging

Each load also has its own message thread, scoped to that load. From the Active Loads tab → click a load → there's a Messages section within the load detail. Messages sent there appear in:

- The load's thread (visible to driver from their load detail).
- The driver's general thread (visible to you in Messages).

This dual-threading is intentional. You can have a load-specific conversation that's easy to find later, while still having the broader driver thread for context.

Use load threads when:
- The conversation is about a specific load (rate dispute, dock issue, ETA update).
- You want the conversation searchable by load later.

Use the general thread for:
- General dispatching ("I'm available", "going off duty", "headed home").
- Cross-load issues.

## What dispatchers message about

A lot of typical reasons:

- **"Are you available for a load?"** Before assigning a non-routine load.
- **"Confirm pickup at 7AM"** when the driver hasn't acknowledged the assignment.
- **"Receiver wants you to call before arriving"** — specific instructions from a broker.
- **"Where are you?"** — when GPS shows them in an unexpected place.
- **"Need an updated ETA for the broker."**
- **"Thanks for the smooth delivery."** — positive feedback. Don't skip this.

## Tone and conventions

Same general rules as any work chat:

- **Be clear.** "Need ETA for LD-3492 ASAP for broker call" beats "what's your eta."
- **Be respectful.** Drivers are professionals. Treat them like it.
- **Be timely.** Same-day responses are the norm. Don't ghost.
- **Use Load IDs.** "LD-3492" is more useful than "the Chicago load."
- **Use names.** "Hey Marcus" is friendlier than "Driver."

A few specific patterns:

- **End-of-shift check-ins.** "How'd today go?" Doesn't take much; drivers appreciate it.
- **Pre-assignment briefings.** "Got a 600-mile pickup tomorrow morning, you good?" Better than just dispatching it blind.
- **Damage reporting.** If a driver photographs damage, acknowledge: "Thanks, I'll let the broker know."

## Photo attachments

Tap the paperclip icon to attach a photo. Useful for:

- **Receiver's rate confirmation** — sometimes you need to share an updated rate con.
- **Lot diagrams** — if a receiver has a complicated approach, photo it.
- **Damage** — share a photo of damage with the driver who's about to pick up so they know what to look for.

Avoid sending memes, jokes, or non-work photos. Drivers expect professionalism on the work channel.

## Bulk announcements

Sometimes you need to message multiple drivers about the same thing — a holiday schedule, a policy update, a broker change. Right now there's no built-in broadcast feature. Options:

1. **Send to each driver individually.** Tedious but reliable.
2. **Use email** — for drivers with current email addresses on file.
3. **Ask Super Admin** to send an in-app announcement (they have the tools).

For routine bulk communication, email or text outside the app is usually faster.

## What drivers see

When you send a message:

- **Their phone shows a badge** on the Messages tab next to the unread count.
- **A banner appears briefly** at the top of their screen if they have the app open.
- **No OS-level push notification** — the app runs in the browser and doesn't have that capability.

In practice, drivers check the app periodically. Same-day response is the norm. Faster than that depends on whether they're driving (they shouldn't be reading messages mid-drive).

## Search and history

You can search within a conversation for specific terms or load IDs. The full message history is preserved — nothing is deleted.

If you need to recover a message from weeks ago, scroll up. If the conversation is very long, the app loads older messages on scroll.

## Privacy and audit

Every message is stored in the database:

- **You** see your conversations.
- **Drivers** see their conversations.
- **Super Admin** can read all conversations for audit purposes.

This isn't a private channel between you and the driver — it's a work channel. Treat it accordingly. Don't share personal info, don't badmouth others, don't put anything in writing you wouldn't want leadership to see.

## When messaging isn't enough

Some situations need a phone call, not a message:

- **Emergencies.** Don't message about a hospital visit; call.
- **Complicated coordination.** Reassignment hand-offs, especially.
- **Sensitive conversations.** Performance issues, disagreements.
- **Anything where back-and-forth typing would waste time.**

Both you and the driver have phone numbers on file. Use them when the situation calls for it.

## After-hours coverage

If your company has after-hours coverage (one dispatcher rotating overnight), the messaging continues to work. The on-duty dispatcher sees and responds to messages.

When you're going off duty, message your drivers proactively: "I'm out till 7 AM tomorrow; for anything urgent overnight, call the after-hours line at XXX-XXX-XXXX or message and someone will respond."

## Pro tips

- **Pin urgent messages.** If you have a driver who's been silent and you've messaged them about something time-sensitive, follow up with a brief call. Don't let it sit.
- **Don't multi-thread for the same issue.** If you're talking about LD-3492 in both the load thread and the general thread, you'll lose track. Pick one and stick with it.
- **Read receipts matter.** If you sent something important and the driver hasn't read it in 30 minutes, follow up or call.
- **Acknowledge driver messages quickly.** Even a "got it, let me check" buys you peace and shows you're paying attention.
