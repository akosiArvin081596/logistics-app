COMMERCIAL VEHICLE LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS COMMERCIAL VEHICLE LEASE AGREEMENT (the "Lease") is entered into and made effective as of the date of the final signature below (the "Effective Date"), by and between LogisX Inc., a Texas corporation, having its principal place of business at 4576 Research Forest Dr, Suite 200, The Woodlands, TX 77381 (the "Lessor"), and the individual or entity identified on the signature page of this Agreement (the "Lessee").

ARTICLE 1: PROPERTY DESCRIPTION, TERM, AND TITLE

1.01 Lease of Equipment. The Lessor hereby leases to the Lessee, and the Lessee hereby leases from the Lessor, the commercial motor vehicle(s) (the "Vehicle" or "Equipment") specifically described by VIN and technical specification in Exhibit A, which is attached hereto and incorporated by reference.

1.02 Term of Lease. The term of this Lease shall commence upon the physical delivery of the Vehicle to the Lessee (the "Commencement Date") and shall continue for an initial term of one (1) year. Upon the expiration of the initial term, this Lease shall automatically convert to a month-to-month arrangement unless either Party provides at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice of intent to terminate.

1.03 Warranty of Condition. The Lessor warrants and represents that, at the time of delivery, the Vehicle is in good working order, is mechanically sound, and meets all Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) and Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) roadworthiness standards. EXCEPT AS SPECIFICALLY PROVIDED HEREIN, THE LESSOR MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING ANY WARRANTY OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

1.04 Retention of Title and Ownership. This Agreement constitutes a contract of leasing only. The Lessee acquires no right, title, or interest in or to the Vehicle except for the right of possession and use as a lessee under the terms of this Agreement. Legal and beneficial title to the Vehicle shall remain exclusively with the Lessor at all times unless transferred via a separate Bill of Sale.

1.05 Protection of Title. The Lessee shall not permit any lien, levy, or legal process to be asserted against the Vehicle. In the event the Lessee receives notice of any claim or lien against the Vehicle, the Lessee shall notify the Lessor in writing within twenty-four (24) hours.

ARTICLE 2: LESSEE COVENANTS AND OPERATIONAL OBLIGATIONS

2.01 Lease Payments and Remittance Structure. The Lessee agrees to pay the Lessor "Rent" as a derivative of the Net Operating Income (NOI) generated by the Vehicle, as defined and governed by the Master Participation & Management Agreement.

  • Variable-Yield Settlement: Disbursements shall be calculated based on the 50/50 profit participation model after all Priority Expenses (Labor, Fuel, Insurance, and Reserves) have been satisfied.
  • Settlement Cycle (Net-60): In alignment with industry receivable aging, all lease payments shall be settled and remitted on the last Friday of the calendar month following the month of production.
  • Method of Payment: Remittance shall be executed via ACH Direct Deposit using the banking coordinates provided by the Lessor in Schedule A (Payment & Banking Election Form).

2.02 Maintenance and Operational Care. The Lessee, at its own expense, shall provide for the preventative maintenance and routine repairs required to keep the Vehicle in professional operating condition.

  • Routine Services: This includes, but is not limited to, oil and filter changes, lubrication, required safety inspections, and interior/exterior cleaning.
  • Tire and Brake Maintenance: The Lessee is responsible for the maintenance and, when operationally necessary, the replacement of tires and brake components. Any replacement parts or tires shall immediately become the property of the Lessor upon installation.
  • Redelivery Standards: Upon termination, the Lessee shall return the Vehicle with tires having no less than 30% remaining tread and brakes having no less than 30% remaining lining.

2.03 Alterations and Major Repairs.

  • Consent for Modifications: The Lessee shall not make any substantial structural changes or mechanical modifications to the Vehicle without the express prior written consent of the Lessor.
  • Major Repair Protocol: The Lessee shall promptly notify the Lessor of any required major repairs. For repairs exceeding the scope of the Maintenance Reserve, the Lessor shall provide specific instructions and shall be responsible for the associated costs, provided the damage did not result from the Lessee's gross negligence or unauthorized use.

2.04 Responsibility for Asset Damage. The Lessee shall be responsible for all damage to the Vehicle while in the Lessee's possession and control, excluding standard wear and tear. Any costs for repairs resulting from driver negligence or inappropriate use shall be assessed against the Net Operating Income (NOI) or the Maintenance Reserve as a Priority Expense.

ARTICLE 3: INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION

3.01 Primary Operational Insurance. During the term of this Lease, the Lessee shall, as a Priority Expense, procure and maintain Commercial Automobile Liability and Property Damage insurance ("Liability Insurance") covering the Vehicle(s) while under active dispatch.

  • Coverage Limits: Such insurance shall provide a Combined Single Limit (CSL) for Bodily Injury and Property Damage of no less than $1,000,000.00 per occurrence, or as required by FMCSA and Texas DOT regulations.
  • Policy Requirements: The policy shall name the Lessor as an Additional Insured. The Lessee shall ensure the policy provides for at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the Lessor before any material alteration or cancellation.
  • Evidence of Coverage: The Lessee shall furnish Certificates of Insurance (COI) to the Lessor prior to the Commencement Date and at least ten (10) days prior to any policy expiration.

3.02 Failure to Maintain. If the Lessee fails to maintain the required Liability Insurance, the Lessor is authorized (but not obligated) to procure such coverage. All costs incurred by the Lessor for such procurement shall be deducted from the Net Operating Income (NOI) or the Maintenance Reserve as an emergency Priority Expense.

3.03 Mutual Indemnification.

  • Operational Indemnity: To the extent not covered by insurance, the Lessee shall release, indemnify, and hold the Lessor harmless from and against any claims, causes of action, or liabilities arising out of the maintenance, use, or operation of the Vehicle while in the Lessee's possession.
  • Personnel Indemnity: The Lessee shall indemnify and hold the Lessor harmless from all claims for death, injury, or property damage sustained by the Lessee's employees, contracted drivers, or agents arising from the operation of the Vehicle.
  • Disclaimer of Control: The Lessor, by being named an additional insured, does not assume any dominion, control, or liability over the daily operation or dispatch of the Vehicle.

ARTICLE 4: PHYSICAL DAMAGE AND RISK OF LOSS

4.01 Responsibility for Physical Loss. The Lessee shall be responsible for all loss or damage to the Vehicle while this Lease is in effect, subject to the following:

  • Covered Perils: This includes, but is not limited to, damage from collision, upset, fire, theft, windstorm, or earthquake.
  • Total Loss Valuation: In the event of a total loss or unrecovered theft, the Lessee shall pay the Lessor the Stated Value or depreciated value of the Vehicle as defined in Exhibit A.

4.02 Physical Damage Coverage. The Lessee shall maintain a policy of Physical Damage Insurance (Comprehensive and Collision) acceptable to the Lessor.

  • Loss Payee Status: The policy shall name the Lessor as the Sole Loss Payee.
  • Deductibles: The deductible shall not exceed the amount specified in Exhibit A. Any deductible owed following a claim shall be treated as a Priority Expense deducted from the Maintenance Reserve or NOI.

ARTICLE 5: ACCIDENT REPORTING AND CARGO LIABILITY

5.01 Immediate Notification Protocol. The Lessee shall notify the Lessor immediately upon the occurrence of any accident, theft, or incident resulting in damage to the Vehicle or involving its use.

  • Method of Communication: Notification must be made via the fastest available means (e.g., telephone or emergency digital dispatch).
  • Driver Obligations: The Lessee shall ensure the driver provides a detailed written report within twenty-four (24) hours and renders all necessary assistance to the Lessor and the insurer in the investigation, defense, or prosecution of any subsequent claims or legal actions.

6.01 Cargo Liability and Indemnification. The Lessee hereby releases, and covenants to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Lessor from any and all liability for loss, damage, or theft of goods, cargo, or other property in the Lessee's custody or control, regardless of where such loss occurs.

6.02 Cargo Insurance Requirements. The Lessee shall, at its sole expense, maintain Cargo Insurance in an amount consistent with industry standards and FMCSA requirements (typically $100,000.00 minimum).

  • Named Insured: The Lessee shall include the Lessor as a Named Insured or Loss Payee on all cargo or transportation floater policies.
  • Evidence of Coverage: A valid Certificate of Insurance showing these endorsements must be maintained on file with the Lessor at all times.

6.03 Waiver of Subrogation. The Lessee expressly waives any legal right of recovery or subrogation against the Lessor for any loss or damage to cargo or property carried on or within the Vehicle.

ARTICLE 7: TAXES, LICENSES, AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

7.01 Responsibility for Taxes. The Lessee shall be responsible for the timely filing and payment of all taxes, fees, and assessments arising from the operation of the Vehicle, which shall be treated as Priority Expenses. This includes:

  • Federal Heavy Vehicle Use Tax (IRS Form 2290).
  • International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) reporting and payments.
  • International Registration Plan (IRP) apportioned registration and plating fees.

7.02 Regulatory Permits. The Lessee shall secure and maintain all necessary state and federal permits required for the legal operation of the Vehicle in interstate commerce.

7.03 Fines and Penalties. The Lessee shall be solely responsible for all fines, citations, and penalties incurred due to equipment violations (e.g., overweight, lighting) or driver conduct. Any such costs paid by the Lessor on the Lessee's behalf shall be deducted from the Net Operating Income (NOI).

ARTICLE 8: MUTUAL COVENANTS AND REGULATORY FILINGS

8.01 Responsibility for Taxes and Licensing. Except as otherwise designated in Exhibit A, the Lessee shall be responsible for the timely application, filing, and payment of all federal, state, and local taxes, licenses, and permits required for the operation of the Vehicle. Any regulatory assessments not specifically allocated to the Lessor shall be the sole responsibility of the Lessee and treated as a Priority Expense.

8.02 Motor Fuel Tax (IFTA) Compliance. The Lessee shall prepare and file all International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) returns and related motor fuel tax reports from its own records.

  • Record Keeping: The Lessee shall maintain and, upon request, provide the Lessor with all trip records, fuel receipts, and mileage logs necessary to audit fuel tax compliance.
  • Accuracy: The Lessee assumes full liability for the accuracy of these filings and any subsequent audits by taxing authorities.

8.03 Governmental Charges and Penalties. The Lessee agrees to pay when due all governmental charges, including but not limited to:

  • Fines and Tolls: All traffic citations, overweight fines, and electronic tolling assessments (e.g., TxTag).
  • Property and Excise Taxes: Any ad valorem or use taxes assessed against the Vehicle during the Term.
  • Default on Taxes: Should the Lessor be required to pay any such charges on the Lessee's behalf, the Lessor shall deduct the full amount plus a 15% administrative fee from the Net Operating Income (NOI).

8.04 Force Majeure. The Lessor shall incur no liability for a failure to supply the Vehicle or any contracted service if prevented by acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, labor disputes, or other causes beyond its reasonable control.

8.05 Operational Limits and Weight Compliance. The Lessee shall ensure that the Vehicle is never operated in excess of the manufacturer's recommended maximum Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW) or Gross Combination Weight (GCW) as specified in Exhibit A.

8.06 Vehicle Specifications and Mandatory Retrofits.

  • Acceptance: The acceptance of the Vehicle into service constitutes an acknowledgment by the Lessee that the Vehicle meets all operational specifications.
  • Mandatory Safety Upgrades: If any federal, state, or local regulation requires the subsequent installation of additional safety or anti-pollution devices (e.g., ELD hardware or emissions sensors), the Manager shall arrange for installation, and the cost shall be treated as a Priority Expense.

8.07 Assignment and Sub-Leasing.

  • Prohibition on Sub-Leasing: The Lessee shall not sub-lease the Vehicle or permit its use by any third party without the prior written consent of the Lessor.
  • Successors: This Lease is binding upon the parties and their respective successors and legal representatives.
  • Material Change: The Lessee shall notify the Lessor in writing of any material change in ownership or disposition of business assets.

8.08 Entire Agreement and Amendments. This Lease, together with the Master Participation & Management Agreement, constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties. No verbal agreements or informal writings shall alter these terms. All amendments must be executed in writing by authorized representatives of both the Lessor and the Lessee.

8.09 Formal Notice. Any notice required under this Lease shall be delivered personally, via guaranteed overnight courier, or by certified mail to the addresses specified in the preamble of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 9: CANCELLATION, TERMINATION, AND DEFAULT

9.01 Termination for Convenience. Either the Lessor or the Lessee may terminate this Lease, in whole or with respect to specific Vehicles, at any time by providing thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

  • Partial Termination: This Lease may be canceled in part by specifying certain Vehicles for removal under the same notice terms, while the Lease remains in full effect for all other Vehicles.
  • Redelivery: Upon termination, the Lessee shall promptly return the Vehicle(s) to a location designated by the Lessor. The Vehicle(s) must be returned in roadworthy condition, complete, and in good order, reasonable wear and tear excepted.

9.02 Automatic Termination and Event of Default. This Agreement shall terminate automatically at the election of the Lessor, and without prior notice to the Lessee, upon the occurrence of any of the following "Events of Default":

  • The Lessee becomes insolvent or is adjudicated bankrupt;
  • The Lessee files a voluntary petition in bankruptcy or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors;
  • A receiver is appointed for the Lessee's business or assets;
  • The Lessee undergoes a material liquidation of business assets;
  • Any of the Lessee's property is subjected to levy, seizure, or sale by any creditor or government agency.

9.03 Governing Law and Jurisdiction. This Lease shall be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas. The Parties hereby irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state or federal courts located in Montgomery County, Texas, for the resolution of any disputes arising from or relating to this Lease.
